About the Roadmap

About the Digital Literacy and Skills Roadmap

This national Roadmap is a guide for building digital literacy and skills for Canada to be used by education, industry, government, individuals and interested stakeholders. The model is intended to be flexible and fluid and areas may cross-over while also offering a continuum. To navigate the tool, it is suggested to start with the “Discover” Stage and work your way through the document to the “Lead” Stage. The “Discover” Stage is the starting point for building the foundation for digital literacy and skills. This tool is intended to be inclusiveness as digital is everywhere. This is why, under Learning Pathways, you will note 3 pathways – Education, Occupation and Personal.  The education path introduces digital to learners through education; whereas the occupation path introduces digital to workers and the citizen path introduces digital to individuals and society.

The Roadmap was built to be continually evergreened as digital literacy and skills evolve. We invite you to share feedback, insights, additional explanations, and skills definitions to inform the pathways and the Roadmap.

The Roadmap includes a compilation and elements of various digital literacy models, including the extensive work done by MediaSmarts, ESDC, ISTE, ISED, and other digital literacy experts, and has been built by ICTC’s Canadian Leadership Taskforce on Education & Skills.

Pathway: Educational

This is an education/training development path where individuals will learn and acquire digital literacy and digital skills through some form of formal education/ training. In some circumstances, they may be self-taught.

Early Stages to Grade 3

Abilities

  • Start to build foundational thinking skills for use of technology (no/limited technology required).
  • Understand underlying principles that encompass digital literacy.
  • Application of growing literacies (reading, writing, numeracy in and through technology-rich environments).

Grades 3 to 6

Abilities

  • Access & Use technology

Digital Skills

  • Foundation
  • Complementary

Foundation Skills Title

Foundation Skills Definition

Use

  • Find and select information online.
  • Access online tools and resources and navigate with some ease.
  • Interact online/ participate in networks.
  • Share resources through online tools .

Understand

  • Begin to understand using and sharing of intellectual property and personal data and ethical online practices.
  • Begin to evaluate and verify information online.
  • Start to understand the consequences of the networked nature of technology.
  • Use technology to inform learning.
  • Show understanding of the concept that online actions can have real consequences.
  • Begin to understand fundamental concepts of technology operations, demonstrate the ability to choose, use and troubleshoot current technologies.

Create

  • Begin to use digital technology to create practical and creative products.
  • With support, begin to use digital technology to communicate with family and friends.

Complementary Skills Title

Complementary Skills Definition

  • Learn how to read and follow instructions to complete a task (building logic).
  • Begin to learn how to break a problem into parts or steps and create instructions – tied to problem-solving & analytical skills development.
  • Begin to develop skills for promoting mental wellness in a digital context.
  • Start to understand the ways in which digital technology affects communication.
  • Manage relationships and deal with conflict online.

Grades 6 to 8

Abilities

  • Build understanding & knowledge; evaluate; curate; information process

Digital Skills

  • Foundation
  • Complementary
  • Core/ICT
  • Advanced

Foundation Skills Title

Foundation Skills Definition

Use

  • Access/interact online and navigate with complete ease across devices

Understand

  • Critical/analytical thinking - Start to analyse, question, deconstruct problems or lack of understanding toward solutions.
  • Understand and practice digital/online privacy and safety.
  • Understand ethical issues when accessing and creating digital content.
  • Begin to understand the methods and purposes of data collection online.

Create

  • Participate in online spaces with an awareness of the permanence and networked nature of digital technology.

Complementary Skills Title

Complementary Skills Definition

  • Be able to communicate/explain basic ideas clearly to various audiences.
  • Apply skills to promote mental wellness in a digital context.
  • Abstract thinking/ Deduction – learning to reduce clutter/ unnecessary information.
  • Take active steps to promote positive interactions and healthy relationships online.
  • Demonstrate the ability to work collaboratively in technology-rich environments.

Core/ICT Skills Title

Core/ICT Skills Definition

Use

  • Identify, locate, retrieve, store, organise and analyse digital information, judging its relevance and purpose (i.e. Information processing and info management skills, etc.)
  • Expand learning across tools, platforms, technologies to build knowledge.

Understand

  • Cultural and social understanding.
  • Understand the fundamental concepts of technology operations, demonstrate the ability to choose, use and troubleshoot current and emerging technologies.
  • Comprehend, contextualize, and critically evaluate digital media, so that we can make informed decisions about what we do and encounter online.

Create

  • Develop basic coding skills for a specific task or self-support technical issues or customize software packages.

Advanced Skills Title

Advanced Skills Definition

Create

  • Finding and fixing technology errors/bugs.
  • Experiment with various technologies to build technology/digital solutions/products.

Grade 8 to Post-Secondary

Abilities

  • Apply essential complementary skills and knowledge for career, personal and/or creative endeavours within technology-rich environments.

Digital Skills

  • Complementary
  • Core/ICT
  • Advanced

Complementary Skills Title

Complementary Skills Definition

  • Understand how legislation, regulation and corporate ownership influence technology and digital spaces.
  • Develop personal strategies to promote mental wellness and screen/life balance in a digital context.
  • Actively contribute to positive social norms in the workplace and/or in online communities as digital citizens.

Core/ICT Skills Title

Core/ICT Skills Definition

Use

  • Individually and/or collaboratively solve conceptual problems through digital means, creatively use technologies.
  • Individually and/or collaboratively use digital technology effectively for personal, creative and professional purposes.

Understand

  • Analyse/evaluate and make informed decisions on tools, platforms, technologies to meet various needs.
  • Understand their rights and responsibilities when using and creating digital media.
  • Take active steps to control the collection of one’s personal data online.

Create

  • Take active steps towards building a positive online identity.

Advanced Skills Title

Advanced Skills Definition

Use

  • Use digital technology for civic engagement.

Understand

  • Understand how a set of problems can be solved (i.e. algorithms) and how they may reflect the biases and assumptions of their creators.

Create

  • Create and/or remix content for personal, creative and professional purposes using digital technology.

or Vocational Training

  • Identify and solve problems and/or create new, useful solutions that leverage technology to support innovation.
  • Demonstrate entrepreneurship in technology-rich environments.

Digital Skills

Complementary Skills Definition

  • Understand how business processes intersect with technology.
  • Be able to explain complex ideas clearly.
  • Exhibit a tolerance for ambiguity, perseverance and the capacity to work with open-ended problems.
  • Apply an understanding of the features of positive communities and healthy relationships as digital creators.

Core/ICT Skills Title

Core/ICT Skills Definition

Understand

  • Develop a deep understanding of languages and constructs: principles of programming.
  • Analyse/evaluate and make informed decisions on tools, platforms, technologies to meet various needs.

Create

  • Design, create and edit digital content and/or new products/ resources using technology for varying audiences.
  • Solve conceptual problems through digital means, creatively use technologies.

Advanced Skills Title

Advanced Skills Definition

Use

  • Formulate technology-assisted problem definitions i.e. data analysis, abstract models and algorithmic thinking in exploring and finding solutions.
  • Define and describe architecture, components, modules, user and technical interfaces, and data for a system to satisfy specified requirements.
  • Solve technical / digital problems.

Understand

  • Understand how automation works and use algorithmic thinking to develop sequence of steps to create & test automated solutions.
  • Maintain and manage systems infrastructure and applications in the delivery of products and services to user population.
  • Follow the trends and applications of the “Internet of Things".

Create

  • Build technology architecture/ platforms.
  • Create digital products, solutions, technologies - develop, test and refine prototypes as part of design/development cycle.

Abilities

  • Collaborate and mentor others.
